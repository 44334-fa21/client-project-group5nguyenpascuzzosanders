OUTPATIENT THERAPY

OUTPATIENT THERAPY

Therapy is an excellent resource for individuals facing a myriad of challenges ranging from depression or anxiety to trauma- and everything in between. Our licensed staff is trained to work with individuals, couples, and families. Types of therapy offered include Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, EMDR, Play Therapy, and more.

Where can I get this service?

Clarinda, Red Oak, Shenandoah, & at home via telehealth!

Who's eligible to receive this service?

This service is available to adults and children of all ages, from any community, that are capable of attending appointments onsite, or from their homes via telehealth during standard business hours.

MEDICATION MANAGEMENT

What is medication management?
Our licensed and highly qualified Medication Management team offers assessments, prescriptions, and ongoing care/monitoring for individuals taking psychiatric medications.

Where can I get this service?

Clarinda, Red Oak, Shenandoah, Villisca (in collaboration with Villisca Medical Clinic), & at home via telehealth!

Who's eligible to receive this service?

This service is available to adults and children of all ages, from any community, that are capable of attending appointments onsite, or from their homes via telehealth during standard business hours.

COMMUNITY SUPPORT SERVICES (CSS)

What is CSS?

Community Support Services provides home and community-based support to individuals living with serious, persistent mental illness. Together with their assigned Community Support Specialist, clients in this program work on setting, meeting, and maintaining their wellness goals!

Where can I get this service?

Page, Fremont, and Montgomery Counties

Who's eligible to receive this service?

This service is available to adults living in the counties listed above, with a history of psychiatric treatment more intensive than outpatient care, more than once in their lifetime, that are in need of assistance/additional supports in order to function in their community. Iowa Medicaid or regional funding are required for eligibility.

SUPPORTED COMMUNITY LIVING

What is CSS?

Like CSS, Supported Community Living also provides home and community-based support to individuals living with serious, persistent mental illness. Together with their assigned Supported Community Living Worker, clients in this program work to develop coping and life skills needed to participate more fully in their communities.

Where can I get this service?

Page, Fremont, and Montgomery Counties

Who's eligible to receive this service?

This service is available to adults living in the counties listed above, with a history of psychiatric treatment more intensive than outpatient care, more than once in their lifetime, that are in need of assistance/additional supports in order to function in their community. Iowa Medicaid or regional funding are required for eligibility.

INTEGRATED HEALTH HOME (IHH)

What is IHH?

Our Integrated Health Home (IHH) is a team of professionals, including family and peer support services, that work together to provide whole-person, patient-centered, coordinated care for adults with a serious mental illness (SMI) and children with a serious emotional disturbance (SED). Our Care Coordinators help IHH members to meet individual goals related to their healthcare needs- and will work with members' to provide information and resources required to assist members with healthy living.

Where can I get this service?

Page, Fremont, & Montgomery Counties

Who's eligible to receive this service?

This service is available to children and adults enrolled in Iowa Medicaid and residing in one of the above listed counties.

PEER SUPPORT SERVICES

What are Peer Support Services?

Peer Support Services offer opportunities for learning and skill building in a peer-driven setting. Our Peer Support Services offer a dynamic mix of groups, activities, and community outings, facilitated by a certified Peer Support Specialist with lived recovery experience. ​​

Where can I get this service?

Peer Support Services are currently offered in:
  • Clarinda (Tuesdays, 12:30pm-4pm)
  • Red Oak (Wednesdays, 12:30pm-4pm)
  • Shenandoah (Thursdays, 12:30pm-4pm)
Transportation to Hope Center events and activities may be available through IA Medicaid. Call us to learn more! Who's eligible to receive this service? Any adult with Iowa Medicaid or regional funding. Call our offices for more info!
